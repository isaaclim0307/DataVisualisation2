Population Growth

Introduction

Population Growth refers to the annual average rate of change of population size for a given country during a specific period. Population Growth can be determined by the birth rate and the death rate of a certain country. If the death rate is higher than the birth rate, population size of that country will be smaller in that year. The first idiom is a map which shows the total population for each country. The second idiom is a bubble plot which display the birth rate and death rate for each country. The third idiom is a line chart which compares the death rate of Australia and Malaysia from year 2000 to 2021. The last idiom is a stacked bar chart which shows the total number of deaths for male and female categorized by age group in year 2020.
